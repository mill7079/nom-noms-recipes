Chicken Pot Pie

Total time: 1 hour | Serves: 8

Ingredients

  • 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast halves - cubed*
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup sliced carrots**
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 cup frozen green peas
  • 1/4 teaspoon celery seed
  • 1/2 cup sliced celery**
  • 1 3/4 cups chicken broth
  • 1/3 cup butter
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1/3 cup chopped onion
  • 2 (9-inch) unbaked pie crusts (or crescent rolls)
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
*or, use one or two (sweet) potatoes

**alternatively, use 1.5ish cups frozen mixed vegetables

Preparation

  1. In a saucepan, combine chicken (or potato), carrots, peas, and celery. Add water to cover and boil for 15 minutes. Remove from heat, drain, and set aside.
  2. In the saucepan over medium heat, cook onions in butter until soft and translucent. Stir in flour, salt, pepper, and celery seed. Slowly stir in chicken broth and milk. Simmer over medium-low heat until thick. Remove from heat and set aside.
  3. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F.
  4. Place the chicken mixture in the bottom pie crust. Pour hot liquid mixture over. Cover with top crust (or crescent rolls), seal edges, and cut away excess dough. Make several small slits in the top to allow steam to escape.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 to 35 minutes, or until pastry is golden brown and filling is bubbly. Cool for 10 minutes before serving.
