Biscuits

Total time: ~2.5 hours | Yield: 12+ biscuits | Oven Temp: 375F

Ingredients

  • 4 cups biscuit or all purpose flour plus more for dusting
  • 4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cubed, very cold
  • 1 cup plus 2 tablespoons buttermilk
  • butter, melted, for brushing

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees Fahrenheit and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Using your hands or a pastry cutter, incorporate the butter cubes into the dry ingredients, leaving large chunks.
  4. Fold in the buttermilk until a thick dough forms. Dough will likely be crumbly.
  5. Lightly flour a clean surface and dump the dough onto it. Bring the dough together until it comes together in one large piece.
  6. Roll our or press to about 1.5-2 inches thick. Using a biscuit cutter or small glass, cut out 2-3 inch rounds and place them on the prepared baking sheet. It's okay if the biscuits are touching.
  7. Bake for 18-20 minutes or until golden brown. In my experience, they get a bit crunchy when the top starts getting golden brown, and that's also way over 20 minutes, so stick closer to 20 minutes.
  8. Brush with melted butter and serve. Good with honey, more butter, or alone.
