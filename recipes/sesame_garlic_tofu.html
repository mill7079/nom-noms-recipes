Sesame Garlic Tofu

Total time: 1 hour | Yield: 3 servings

Ingredients for Tofu

  • 1 package extra firm tofu
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Rice and/or vegetables to serve with

Ingredients for Sauce

  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 2 teaspoons honey
  • dash chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Preparation

  1. Press the tofu for 30 minutes. Once the tofu has drained, slice in half then into cubes and place into a large bowl. Sprinkle cornstarch on top and gently toss with your hands to ensure all tofu pieces are covered.
  2. In a large skillet, add sesame oil and vegatable oil then bring up to medium-high heat. Once the oil is heated up, add the tofu and garlic to the skillet and let brown on all sides.
  3. In the meantime, whisk together the ingredients for the sauce. Once the tofu has browned and crisped up on all sides, add the sauce to the skillet and it should start to thicken immediately. Toss the tofu around to coat then remove from heat.
  4. Serve with rice and/or vegetables and enjoy!
