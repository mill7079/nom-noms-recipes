Gingersnaps

Total time: 1-2 hours | Yield: 4-5 dozen small cookies | Oven temp: 350F

Ingredients

  • 2 cups sifted all purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon ground ginger
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon (I usually add extra, closer to 1.5 tsp)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (if Diamond Crystal, 1 teaspoon)
  • 3/4 cup shortening
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup dark molasses
  • 1/3 cup cinnamon sugar

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.
  2. Sift the flour, ginger, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt into a small mixing bowl. Stir to mix evenly.
  3. Place the shortening into a medium mixing bowl and beat until creamy. Gradually beat in the white sugar; once creamed, beat in the egg and the molasses.
  4. Add 1/3 of the flour mixture to the shortening mixture and stir to thoroughly blend. Add the remaining flour mixture and mix until a soft dough forms.
  5. Pinch off small amounts of dough, roll into balls with a 1 inch diameter, roll in the cinnamon sugar, and place 2 inches apart on an ungreased baking sheet (parchment paper works great).
  6. Bake until the tops are rounded and cracked, about 10 minutes. Cool for two minutes on the pan before transferring to a wire rack.
