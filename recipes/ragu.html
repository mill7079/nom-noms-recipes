Charred Vegetable Ragu

Total time: 1-3 hours, probably | Yield: 4-6 servings | Requires food processor, broiler

Ingredients

  • 4 large portobello mushrooms, stemmed and quartered
  • 1 garlic head
  • 1 yellow onion, peeled and quartered
  • 2 celery stalks, cut into thirds
  • 2 carrots, peeled and cut into thirds (or smaller, depending on the size of carrot)
  • 6 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, divided
  • 8 ounces fresh crimini mushrooms, stemmed and quartered
  • 1/2 cup tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons dried oregano
  • 1/2 cup dry red wine
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 12 ounces uncooked bronze-cut durum wheat tagliatelle pasta (or whatever fucking pasta you want, but long noodles are best)
  • 1/2 cup grated parmesan, plus more for serving

Preparation

  1. Preheat boiler to low with oven rack in middle of oven.
  2. Toss together portobellows, garlic, onion, celery, carrots, and 1 tablespoon oil on a large rimmed baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Spread in a single layer, and broil in preheated oven until vegetables are charred in spots, about 20 minutes, rotating pan and flipping vegetables halfway through. Let cool 5 minutes.
  3. After cooling, squeeze the roasted garlic out of their skins (discarding the skins) before transferring garlic and vegetables to a food processor. Pulse until finely chopped, about 4 times (potentially more if the carrots are firm); set aside.
  4. Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a large, deep skillet over medium high. Add cremini mushrooms; cook until bottoms of mushrooms are browned, about 3 minutes. Stir and cook until browned on all sides, 5 to 8 minutes.
  5. Stir in tomato paste and oregano; cook, stirring constantly, for 1 minute. Add wine and cook, stirring constantly, until slightly thickened, about 1 minute. Stir in roasted vegetable mixture; season with salt and pepper before removing from heat.
  6. Cook your pasta in salted water, and reserve 1.5 cups of the cooking liquid when draining. Transfer cooked pasta to skillet with sauce, and return to heat over medium.
  7. Stir in reserved cooking liquid, 1/2 cup at a time, until sauce is creamy, about 3 minutes. Season with salt to taste. Serve topped with grated cheese.
