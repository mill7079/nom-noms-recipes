Ramen Eggs

Total time: overnight | Yield: 2

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin or rice vinegar or white wine
  • 1 tablespoon sugar, if using mirin substitue

Preparation

  1. In a resealable plastic bag, combine soy sauce and mirin (and maybe sugar). If you’re planning to let it marinate for a long time, add a few tablespoons of water.
  2. Bring water to a boil in a medium saucepan. There should be enough water to cover the eggs (1 inch above the eggs).
  3. When boiling, take out the eggs from the refrigerator and carefully and slowly submerge the eggs into the boiling water with a mesh strainer/skimmer or ladle to prevent the eggs from cracking.
  4. Immediately reduce heat to maintain a simmer and cook the eggs for exactly 7 minutes (6-6 ½ minutes for a runny yolk, 8-9 minutes for a pretty hard yolk). After 7 minutes, immediately take out the eggs and soak in the ice bath to stop them from cooking further. Let them cool for 3 minutes.
  5. The soft boiled eggs are not completely hardened, so gently peel the eggs and place in the sauce bag. Let them marinate overnight to up to 3-4 days in the refrigerator.
