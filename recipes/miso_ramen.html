Miso Ramen

Total time: 1 hour | Serves: 2

Ingredients for Ramen Soup

  • 2 cloves garlic (1 1/2 teaspoon minced)
  • 1 shallot
  • 1 knob ginger
  • 1 tablespoon toasted white sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/4 pound ground pork
  • 1 teaspoon spicy chili bean sauce/broad bean paste/black bean sauce
  • 3 tablespoons miso
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon sake (optional)
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon white pepper

Ingredients for Ramen and Toppings

  • 2 servings fresh ramen noodles
  • ramen eggs
  • frozen or canned corn
  • nori (seaweed)
  • green onion

Preparation

  1. Mince the garlic and ginger. Mince the shallot.
  2. In a medium pot, heat sesame oil over medium-low heat and add the minced garlic, ginger, and shallot. With a wooden spatula, stir fry until fragrant.
  3. Add the meat and increase heat to medium. Cook the meat until no longer pink.
  4. Add the spicy bean paste and miso. Quickly blend well with the meat before they get burnt.
  5. Add the ground sesame seeds and sugar, mix well. Add sake and chicken stock and bring to a simmer. Taste soup and add (if necessary) salt and white pepper. Cover with lid and keep ramen soup simmering while you cook the noodles.
  6. Bring large pot of unsalted water to a boil. Prepare ramen toppings ahead of time so you can serve fresh ramen immediately.
  7. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions. When the noodles are done, quickly pick them up with a mesh sieve. Make sure to drain the water well. Serve the noodles into bowls, and add the ramen soup and top with the prepared toppings.
