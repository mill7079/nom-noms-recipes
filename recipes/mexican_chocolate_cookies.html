Mexican Chocolate Cookies

Total time: 45 minutes | Yield: 36 cookies

Ingredients for Cookies

  • 1 cup softened butter
  • 1.5 cups white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 0.5 teaspoon sea salt
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons cream of tartar
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 0.5 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon*
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper*
  • 1/4 teaspoon chili powder*
  • 2.25 cups flour
  • Chocolate chips (1-2 cups) (DO NOT USE CHOPPED CHILI CHOCOLATE) (YOU WILL DIE)

Ingredients for Topping

  • 0.25 cup white sugar
  • 0.5 teaspoon cinnamon
  • Some cayenne/chili powder, if desired
* Measurements are approximate; the recipe calls for half of these (and none of the chili powder) but I usually add dashes of each at each step and then the recipe's stated amount at step 4

Preparation

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F and line 2 baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Beat sugar, butter, vanilla, and salt together in a bowl until fluffy.
  3. Beat in eggs.
  4. Beat in cream of tartar, baking soda, cocoa powder, cinnamon, cayenne, and chili powder.
  5. Beat in flour. Dough will be very stiff and hard to stir.
  6. Stir chocolate chips into dough.
  7. Combine topping ingredients in a small bowl and mix thoroughly.
  8. Roll heaping tablespoons of dough into balls, then roll the balls in the topping and place on the prepared baking sheets.
  9. Bake in the preheated oven until centers are set and edges crack slightly, about 10 minutes. Leave on baking sheets for 5 minutes before removing to wire racks.
