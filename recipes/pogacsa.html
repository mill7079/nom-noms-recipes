Pogacsa

Total time: 45 minutes | Yield: 5 dozen

Ingredients for Dough

  • 1/2 cup whole milk, heated to 110 degrees F
  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 4 cups all purpose flour
  • 5 ounces gruyere cheese, finely shredded
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 2 eggs
  • 14 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup sour cream

Ingredients for Topping

  • 1 egg yolk
  • 2 ounces gruyere cheese, finely shredded

Preparation

  1. In a large bowl, combine milk, yeast, and sugar and let stand until yeast is softened, about 10 minutes.
  2. Add flour, cheese, salt, eggs, butter, and sour cream to the yeast mixture. Using an electric mixer, mix on low speed until dough comes together. Beat on medium speed 1 to 2 minutes. The dough should be smooth and not sticky.
  3. Roll dough ½ inch thick on a lightly floured surface. Make a shallow cross-hatched pattern with the point of a sharp knife over the top of the dough and brush with egg yolk. Sprinkle cheese on top, and cut out rounds with a 1 to 1 ½ inch cutter.
  4. Arrange circles in rows on a parchment-lined baking sheet, about ¼ inch apart. Put pan in a cold oven and set temperature to 400 degrees F. Bake about 25 minutes, until pogacsa are nicely browned on their tops and bottoms. Let cool completely and store airtight.
