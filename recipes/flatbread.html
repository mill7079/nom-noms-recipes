Flatbread

Total time: 1 hour | Yield: 6 flatbreads

Ingredients

  • 3 cups flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder (if you want savory)
  • 3/4 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 3/4 cup milk, divided

Preparation

  1. Whisk flour, baking powder, salt, and garlic powder together in a bowl. Make a well in the center. Add yogurt, oil, and ½ cup of milk. Mix with a wooden spoon so that the milk absorbs the flour. Gradually mix in the remaining milk if the mixture is too dry or crumbly. Bring the dough together with your hands. The dough should be workable but sticky to the touch.
  2. Turn dough out onto a lightly floured work surface. Sprinkle the top of the dough with a little extra flour if too sticky. Knead to form a smooth, soft, and semi-sticky ball, about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Return dough to lightly oiled bowl, rotating the dough twice in the oil to evenly coat. Cover with plastic wrap. Set aside to rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Knead dough a few more times until smooth. You shouldn’t need any extra flour at this point to knead, as the oil from the dough will help you.
  5. Divide dough into 6 equal balls. Working one piece at a time, flatten one ball out with the palms of your hands. Use a rolling pin to roll it to about 5mm thick.
  6. Lightly grease a nonstick pan with a drizzle of olive oil over medium heat (ok, I don't actually grease the pan and my pan isn't nonstick -- when I do it there's enough oil on the flatbreads to not stick to the pan, and it's easier to get the right amount of oil by just giving them a nicely oiled bowl in step 3 than putting oil on the pan each time). Cook one flatbread for 2 minutes on one side. Flip and cook until golden, lightly pressing down with a spatula when it puffs up (do they puff up? Because mine never have but they're delightful).
  7. While one piece is cooking, roll the next piece out and repeat steps above.
